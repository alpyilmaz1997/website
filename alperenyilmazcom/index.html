University Studies

ACADEMIC STUDIES

MODELING ANTIBIOTIC RESISTENCE GENES IN BACTERIA BY USING BIOINFORMATICS MOTHODS

GRADUATION PROJECT THESIS
ABSTRACT

Antibiotics are drugs used in treatment of infectious diseases caused by bacte- ria. As a result of its unconscious use by humans; antibiotic resistance has been formed in bacteria, hence antibiotics have lost their effects. In this research, bio- informatics studies on beta – lactam and vancomycin resistance genes have been carried out in order to help find solutions to antibiotic resistance in bacteria. Furthermore, this research is intended to contribute to discovery of new drugs. Similarities of the resistance genes were considered for the purpose of creating a model. Two different modeling approaches were used - Hidden Markov Model and Convolutional Neural Network. Hidden Markov Model is a highly effective way of modeling data that comes in time series, while the Convolutional Neural Network is the most popular and effective method in image classification. MATLAB and Python programming languages were used for these methods. Main goal was to obtain an advanced method by comparing the results from two aforementioned modeling methods.
